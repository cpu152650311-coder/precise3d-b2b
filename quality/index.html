QC Process Overview

Three-Stage Quality Assurance

Quality is not inspected at the end — it is built in at every stage. Our three-stage system catches defects at their source, not after assembly.

IQC — Incoming QC

Every batch of raw materials and components inspected before entering the production floor. Aluminum extrusions, electronic components, motors, bearings, and filament all verified against published specifications. Sampling per AQL 1.0 Level II. Non-conforming lots rejected at the dock and returned to supplier within 24 hours.

IPQC — In-Process QC

Multiple inspection checkpoints embedded directly on the assembly lines. Go/no-go fixtures at each station for rapid pass/fail decisions. First-article inspection for every production batch. Real-time SPC data collection with corrective action triggers at 2-sigma deviation. Six IPQC stations ensure no defect propagates downstream.

OQC — Outgoing QC

Pre-shipment audit on every batch. Random sampling per AQL 0.65 Level II (tightened inspection). Functional re-test on sampled units: print test, connectivity, touchscreen, enclosure safety. Packaging integrity verified. Certificate of conformance issued. Test print log and QC checklist included in each box.

The 7 Inspection Gates

No Step Skipped, No Shortcut Taken

From raw material delivery to palletized shipment, every printer passes through seven controlled inspection gates. Each gate requires operator sign-off and is traced by serial number.

G1

IQC — Incoming Quality Control

Every batch of raw materials inspected before release to production. Aluminum extrusions checked for straightness (0.1mm/m max deviation). Electronic components sampled per AQL 1.0 normal inspection, Level II. Filament spools measured for diameter consistency (1.75mm +/-0.02mm) using laser micrometer. Non-conforming materials quarantined in designated rejection zone. Supplier corrective action report (SCAR) issued within 48 hours for any rejection.

Equipment: Laser micrometer, digital calipers, surface flatness gauge, LCR meter, tensile tester

G2

IPQC-1 — Frame & Motion System

After frame assembly, every unit undergoes geometry verification. Gantry squareness checked to within 0.05 degrees using digital angle gauge. Rail parallelism measured with dial indicator across full travel length. Belt tension verified at 50 Hz +/-5 Hz on acoustic tension meter. All linear bearings tested for smooth travel — any binding or roughness triggers immediate rework. Frame mounting surface flatness verified on granite inspection plate.

Equipment: Digital angle gauge, dial indicator (0.001mm resolution), acoustic belt tension meter, granite surface plate (Grade 0)

G3

IPQC-2 — Electronics & Wiring

Controller board powered on and self-test diagnostics run. All stepper driver channels verified for correct current output. Heated bed resistance measured to within 5% of nominal. Thermistor accuracy checked against calibrated reference to within 1 degree C at three temperature points (20, 100, 200 degrees C). Enclosure safety interlock switches tested for positive break. USB, Ethernet, and Wi-Fi connectivity verified. All connectors pull-tested for secure engagement.

Equipment: Digital multimeter (6.5-digit), thermal reference calibrator, network analyzer, hipot tester

G4

IPQC-3 — Calibration Verification

Auto bed leveling mesh verified across full 5x5 probe grid. Maximum deviation must be under 0.1mm across the entire build plate surface. Z-offset calibrated using feeler gauge method for consistent first layer. Extruder e-steps calibrated: 100mm extrusion command must deliver 100mm +/-0.5mm of filament. PID auto-tune performed and verified for both hotend and heated bed. All calibration values saved to EEPROM and logged per serial number.

Equipment: Feeler gauge set, digital scale (0.01g resolution), infrared thermal camera, calibration reference filament

G5

Test Print — 100% of Units

Every single printer runs a standardized test print — no exceptions. Test suite includes: dimensional accuracy cube (20mm nominal, must measure 19.95-20.05mm), bridging test (50mm span with no sag), overhang test (0-60 degrees, clean surface required through 50 degrees), and retraction stringing test. Professional series printers additionally run a 4-hour continuous print test. All test prints are measured, photographed, and archived by serial number. A copy of the test print is included in the box.

Equipment: Digital microscope (50x), CMM (coordinate measuring machine), test print reference library, standardized filament

G6

Final Inspection

Cosmetic inspection of all exterior surfaces: enclosure panels, frame finish, touchscreen, logo application. Touchscreen function verified: all menu items accessible, touch response calibrated. All ports tested: USB host, USB device, Ethernet, Wi-Fi (connection and transfer test). SD card slot read/write verified. Accessories checklist confirmed against packing list: spool holder, toolkit, USB drive with software and manuals, power cord (region-specific), filament sample, spare nozzle. Serial number label verified and registered in warranty database.

Equipment: Gloss meter, colorimeter, port test jig, barcode scanner, digital checklist system

G7

OQC — Outgoing Quality Control

Final pre-shipment sampling audit per AQL 0.65 Level II tightened inspection — a stricter standard than normal. Sampled units undergo full functional re-test. Packaging integrity verified: ISTA 3A drop test on random cartons, vibration simulation, compression test. Humidity indicator cards verified within specification. Certificate of conformance generated with batch traceability. Serial numbers cross-referenced against production database. OQC sign-off required before any shipment release.

Equipment: Drop test machine (ISTA 3A), vibration table, compression tester, barcode scanner, QC database terminal

Sampling Methodology

AQL-Based Statistical Sampling

We use ANSI/ASQ Z1.4 (ISO 2859-1) sampling standards for statistical quality control. This ensures representative inspection without 100% destructive testing on components.

Testing Equipment

Metrology Lab & Test Instruments

Our in-house metrology lab is equipped with calibrated instruments for precise dimensional, electrical, and environmental testing. All instruments undergo annual third-party calibration.

Dimensional Metrology

  • Coordinate Measuring Machine (CMM) — 2.5 + L/300 um accuracy
  • Laser Micrometer — 0.001mm resolution for filament diameter
  • Digital Calipers — Mitutoyo Absolute, 0.01mm resolution, calibrated quarterly
  • Dial Indicators — 0.001mm resolution for rail and gantry alignment
  • Granite Surface Plate — Grade 0, 1200x900mm, flatness 3.5 um
  • Digital Height Gauge — 0.001mm resolution, 600mm range

Electrical & Environmental

  • 6.5-Digit Bench Multimeter — Keysight 34465A, calibrated annually
  • 4-Channel Oscilloscope — for stepper driver signal analysis
  • Thermal Camera — FLIR, 320x240 resolution, +/-2 degrees C accuracy
  • Sound Level Meter — Class 2, for verifying sub-45dB operation
  • Hipot Tester — 5kV AC/DC for electrical safety verification
  • Environmental Chamber — -20 to 60 degrees C for thermal cycling tests

Mechanical Testing

  • Belt Tension Meter — Acoustic type, 10-100 Hz range, calibrated monthly
  • Digital Torque Wrench — 0.5-25 Nm, for fastener verification
  • Pull Force Tester — For connector retention and wire crimp testing
  • Drop Test Machine — ISTA 3A compliant, for packaging validation
  • Vibration Table — Simulates truck/air/sea transport conditions
  • Compression Tester — Carton stacking strength verification

Optical & Visual Inspection

  • Digital Microscope — 10x-200x magnification with measurement overlay
  • Video Borescope — For internal cavity inspection of assembled units
  • Gloss Meter — 60-degree geometry for surface finish verification
  • Colorimeter — For enclosure panel color consistency across batches
  • LED Inspection Lamp — 5000K daylight, 2000 lux at work surface
  • UV Inspection Lamp — For conformal coating coverage verification

Continuous Improvement

Defect Tracking & Root Cause Analysis

Quality does not stop at inspection. We track every defect, analyze root causes, and implement permanent corrective actions. Our partners benefit from a system that gets better every quarter.

Digital QC Database

Every inspection result is recorded digitally and linked to serial number. Real-time dashboards display yield rates, defect categories, and trend charts. Partner-accessible QC reports available within 24 hours of request.
