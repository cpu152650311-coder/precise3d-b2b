Kinematics Comparison

CoreXY vs. Bedslinger vs. Delta

Each kinematics architecture has trade-offs. Here is why CoreXY is the professional standard for consumer 3D printers.

CoreXY

Bed only moves on Z-axis (slow, precise). XY gantry driven by two stationary motors via a single belt loop. Low moving mass enables high acceleration with minimal ringing. The gold standard for enclosed consumer printers.

Bedslinger (Prusa-style)

Bed moves in Y, print head in X. Simple mechanical design but bed mass limits Y acceleration. Tall prints can wobble as bed shifts. Good for budget printers but limited at high speed.

Delta

Three arms control the effector via vertical carriages. Extremely fast in Z, very tall build volumes possible. Great for vase-mode prints but calibration is complex and effective XY resolution varies by position.

What Our Technology Means for Your Business

Technical architecture directly impacts your customer experience, support costs, and competitive positioning. Here is how Precise3D's technology choices translate into business advantages for our distribution partners.

Fewer Support Tickets

CoreXY kinematics with auto-bed leveling and filament run-out detection eliminate the top three causes of failed prints. Our partners report 40% fewer tier-1 support calls compared to bedslinger-based competitors — saving money on every unit sold.

Higher ASP, Better Margin

CoreXY enclosed printers command a 30-50% price premium over open-frame bedslingers. The visible build quality — rigid frame, enclosed chamber, quiet operation — justifies the higher price point and reduces price-shopping comparisons.

Future-Proof Platform

Our firmware supports over-the-air updates via Wi-Fi. New features — material profiles, print modes, UI improvements — are delivered without hardware changes. Your customers' printers get better over time, building brand loyalty and repeat purchases.
