Built for Schools

Why Most 3D Printers Fail in Classrooms

School districts have requirements that consumer 3D printers weren't designed to meet. Open-frame printers expose students to 200°C hotends. Noisy stepper drivers disrupt classroom focus. Manual bed leveling frustrates teachers who need a tool, not a project. And PTFE-lined hotends off-gas at temperatures that engineering-grade materials require.

The Precise3D Pro X1 addresses every one of these concerns: fully enclosed with HEPA + activated carbon filtration, sub-50dBA silent mode, auto bed leveling, and an all-metal hotend that doesn't off-gas. It meets the safety requirements that most school district procurement checklists demand.

Education Program

What's Included for Education Partners

Education Pricing

15–25% discount off MSRP for qualifying institutions. Volume pricing for lab deployments of 10+ units. Purchase order and net-30 payment terms available for accredited institutions.

Curriculum Bundle

Ready-to-teach lesson plans: 3D design basics (Tinkercad/Fusion 360), material science fundamentals, and project-based learning modules. Aligned with NGSS and ISTE standards for grades 6–12.

Teacher Training

2-hour virtual training session included with every education order. Covers: printer operation, material selection, troubleshooting common issues, and integrating 3D printing into existing curriculum. Certificate of completion provided.

Extended Warranty

2-year warranty standard for education purchases (vs. 1-year consumer). Includes advance replacement for critical failures. Because a broken printer in week 3 of a semester can't wait 2 weeks for repair.

Spare Parts Kit

Every education order includes a complimentary spare parts kit: 2 nozzles, 1 build plate sheet, 1 silicone sock, 1 PTFE tube. Enough to handle a full semester of student use without downtime.

Grant Support

We provide a grant application template, product specifications sheet, and a letter of support for schools applying for STEM/CTE funding. Many schools fund their lab entirely through Title IV and Perkins grants.
